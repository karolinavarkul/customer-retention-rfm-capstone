# Business Case

## Company Context
The dataset represents transactional sales data from a mid-sized office supply retailer operating across five geographic regions in Myanmar. The company provides a wide range of office-related products, including stationery, electronics, and office furniture.

The primary customer base consists of small-to-medium enterprises that rely on consistent supply purchases to support their operations. As a result, customer retention and repeat purchasing behavior are critical drivers of long-term revenue stability.

The dataset covers transactions from [YEAR–YEAR], enabling temporal analysis of retention patterns.

## Business Problem
Although the company maintains steady overall sales volume, leadership has identified uneven revenue distribution across customers and inconsistent repeat purchasing behavior.

Currently, there is limited visibility into:
- Which customers generate the highest revenue contribution
- Which customers demonstrate strong loyalty
- Which customers show declining engagement and may be at risk of churn

Without structured customer segmentation and retention analysis, marketing initiatives remain broad and reactive rather than data-driven and targeted.

## Objective
- Segment customers based on purchasing behaviour using RFM (recency, frequency, monetary) methodology
- Evaluate revenue concentration across customer segments and geographic regions
- Analyze customer retention trends over-time using cohort analysis
- Provide actionable, data-driven recommendations to improve retention, maximize customer lifetime value, and optimize marketing resource allocation

## Target Audience
This analysis is intended for the company's marketing and leadership teams. The insights will support decision-making related to:
- Customer retention strategy development
- Targeted marketing and re-engagement campaigns
- Regional performance monitoring
- Resource allocation toward high-value customer segments
